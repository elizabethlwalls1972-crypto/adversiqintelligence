## THE 9 COMPREHENSIVE SECTIONS

### 1. IDENTITY & FOUNDATION (Who Are You?)
**Purpose:** Establish your organizational baseline
- Organization basics (legal name, entity type, industry, years in operation)
- Organizational capacity (employees, revenue, EBITDA, segments, market share)
- Structure (decision makers, board, advisors)
- Competition (competitors, advantages, IP, brand, customer concentration)
- Financial stability (credit, debt, liquidity)

**Why This Matters:** No one will work with you if you're not credible. This section proves you exist and have capacity.

---

### 2. MANDATE & STRATEGY (Why Are You Here?)
**Purpose:** Define your vision, strategy, and what success looks like
- Strategic vision (3-5 year outlook, intents, problem statement)
- Objectives & KPIs (measurable targets, timelines)
- Target partner profile (size, industry, geography, capabilities, culture)
- Value proposition (what we bring, what we expect, win-win framework)
- Governance model (JV / Alliance / Equity / License / Distribution)

**Why This Matters:** Without a clear mandate, you can't make decisions. This section forces strategic alignment.

---

### 3. MARKET & CONTEXT (Where Are You Going?)
**Purpose:** Understand the market forces at play
- Market sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM, growth rate, maturity)
- Market dynamics (trends, competition, pricing, technology disruption)
- Geographic context (country, region, city, local market size, regulations)
- Macroeconomic factors (GDP, inflation, currency, interest rates, trade policy)
- Industry-specific dynamics (consolidation, pricing power, switching costs)

**Why This Matters:** Market forces can make or break a deal. You MUST understand local dynamics, not assume.

---

### 4. PARTNERS & ECOSYSTEM (Who Else Is Involved?)
**Purpose:** Evaluate partner fit and stakeholder landscape
- Target counterparties (names, size, capabilities, geography)
- Stakeholder landscape (executive, operational, financial, legal approvals)
- Strategic alignment (our goals vs. partner goals, timeline, risk tolerance)
- Cultural fit (decision speed, hierarchy, innovation, risk aversion, communication)
- Competitive conflicts (do we compete, channel conflict, non-compete issues)
- Relationship dynamics (history, trust, prior dealings, communication cadence)

**Why This Matters:** 70% of partnerships fail due to misalignment. This section forces honest assessment upfront.

---

### 5. FINANCIAL MODEL (The Money)
**Purpose:** Build a complete financial picture
- Investment requirements (capital, type, timing, sources, working capital)
- Revenue model (streams, unit economics, year 1/3/5 revenue, growth rate)
- Cost structure (COGS, OpEx, headcount plan, cost drivers)
- Return analysis (break-even, EBITDA margin, NPV, IRR, payback, ROIC)
- Scenarios (base case, downside -25%, upside +25%)
- Sensitivity analysis (key drivers, impact on IRR)

**Why This Matters:** "The business case is always wrong, but it forces you to think." This section ensures you've modeled the key assumptions.

---

### 6. RISK & MITIGATION (What Could Go Wrong?)
**Purpose:** Systematically identify and manage risks
- Risk register (5+ risks by category: market, operational, financial, legal, relationship)
- Risk quantification (probability %, financial impact $)
- Mitigation plans (how we'll reduce each risk)
- Risk appetite (acceptable level: low/medium/high)
- Contingency planning (if X happens, then do Y)

**Why This Matters:** Risk management separates professionals from amateurs. This section prevents surprise failures.

---

### 7. RESOURCES & CAPABILITY (Can You Execute?)
**Purpose:** Audit your ability to execute
- Executive team (names, backgrounds, criticality, bench strength)
- Organizational capabilities (sales, operations, P&L, international, M&A, change management)
- Technology stack (core engine, platform, integrations, IP, technical debt, scalability)
- Team & talent gaps (what roles are needed, market availability)
- Capability gaps (what we have vs. need vs. can build vs. must acquire)

**Why This Matters:** Great plans fail with weak teams. This section reveals execution risk early.

---

### 8. EXECUTION PLAN (HOW & WHEN?)
**Purpose:** Create a realistic roadmap with clear gates
- Phase 1: Foundation (months 1-3) - key milestones, budget, approvals
- Phase 2: Ramp (months 4-9) - scaling, key decisions, checkpoints
- Phase 3: Scale (months 10-18+) - enterprise operations, final validation
- Go/No-Go criteria (what has to be true to proceed at each gate)
- Critical path (items that can't slip without cascading delays)
- Dependencies (what blocks what, parallel workstreams)

**Why This Matters:** "In the absence of a plan, everyone has a different plan." This section ensures alignment and accountability.

---

### 9. GOVERNANCE & MONITORING (HOW Do You Stay Aligned?)
**Purpose:** Establish ongoing decision-making and metrics
- Steering committee (members, frequency - weekly/monthly/quarterly)
- Decision authority matrix (who decides what)
- Key metrics & dashboards (financial KPIs, operational KPIs, strategic KPIs, health)
- Meeting cadence (how often do we check in, escalation paths)
- Contingency triggers ("If revenue misses >20%, then...")

**Why This Matters:** The deal doesn't end when you sign. This section ensures you stay on track and can adapt.

---

## READINESS SCORING

Once you complete the intake, a validation engine scores your readiness:

### Red Status (< 70% complete)
- **NOT READY** to proceed
- Major sections missing
- **Time to complete:** 8-16 hours (2-3 days)
- High execution risk if you proceed

### Yellow Status (70-90% complete)
- **IN PROGRESS** - good progress
- Some sections fully complete, others need work
- **Time to complete:** 2-4 hours (half day)
- Can proceed with caution if willing to fill gaps during execution

### Green Status (90%+ complete)
- **READY** to proceed with confidence
- All critical sections complete
- Clear understanding of what success looks like
- **Can begin analysis/execution** immediately
